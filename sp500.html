💡 Important Rule for Investing: Play the Long Game

Before you invest a single dollar, remember this golden rule: The money you put into the stock market should not be money you need tomorrow.

  • Not for groceries or daily needs: Your investments are separate from your checking account. Keep your daily living expenses safely out of the stock market.
  • Not for rainy days: Always build an emergency fund (3 to 6 months of living expenses) in a safe, high-yield savings account before investing heavily. If your car breaks down, you shouldn't have to sell your stocks to fix it.
  • Think 5, 10, or 30 years out: The S&P 500 fluctuates daily, weekly, and yearly. True wealth is built by letting your money sit and compound over decades. Set it, forget it, and let time do the heavy lifting!
