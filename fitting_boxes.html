Understanding Volume: Cubes within a Cube

The volume of a 3D rectangular object (or a cube) is the amount of space it takes up. It is measured in cubic units, which can be thought of as the number of smaller \(1 \times 1 \times 1\) unit cubes that can fit perfectly inside the larger shape.

If a smaller cube has a side length of \( s \), its volume is:

$$ V_{small} = s \times s \times s = s^3 $$

If a larger cube has a side length of \( S \), its volume is:

$$ V_{large} = S \times S \times S = S^3 $$

To find out how many small cubes fit into the larger cube, we calculate the ratio of their volumes:

$$ \text{Number of small cubes} = \frac{V_{large}}{V_{small}} = \left( \frac{S}{s} \right)^3 $$

Example Visualized Below:
Let the large cube have side length \( S = 3 \) and the small cube have side length \( s = 1 \).
$$ \text{Total Cubes} = \frac{3^3}{1^3} = \frac{27}{1} = 27 \text{ small unit cubes} $$
