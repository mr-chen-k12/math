Problem 1
In Mr. Jensen's math class, 24 students are seated in 4 rows of desks. The same number of students are in each row. Write and solve a division equation to find the number of students in each row.

Equation: \( 24 \div 4 = s \)

Step-by-step: Divide the total number of students (24) by the number of rows (4).

Answer: 6 students

Problem 2
An art teacher works with 42 different students each day. During the school year, each student will complete 9 art projects. Write and solve a multiplication equation to find the total number of projects the students will complete.

Equation: \( 42 \times 9 = p \)

Step-by-step: \[ \begin{array}{r@{\quad}l} 42 & \\ \times 9 & \\ \hline 378 & \end{array} \] (\(2 \times 9 = 18\), carry the 1. \(4 \times 9 = 36\), plus 1 is 37.)

Answer: 378 projects

Problem 3
Write and solve a subtraction equation to find the number of years from 1921 to 1938.

Equation: \( 1938 - 1921 = y \)

Step-by-step: \[ \begin{array}{r} 1938 \\ - 1921 \\ \hline 17 \end{array} \]

Answer: 17 years

Problem 4
How many years is 5 decades?
A) 5 years   B) 50 years   C) 500 years   D) 5000 years

Reasoning: One decade is equal to 10 years.

\( 5 \text{ decades} \times 10 \text{ years/decade} = 50 \text{ years} \)

Answer: B (50 years)

Problem 5
According to this calendar (December 1957), what day of the week was December 25, 1957?

Reasoning: Looking at the calendar grid:

  • Dec 1st is a Sunday.
  • Adding 7 days repeatedly: Dec 8, 15, and 22 are also Sundays.
  • Count forward from Sunday the 22nd:
    • Dec 23 = Monday
    • Dec 24 = Tuesday
    • Dec 25 = Wednesday

Answer: Wednesday

Problem 6
Round 5236 to the nearest thousand.

Answer: 5,000

Problem 10
When T'Von emptied his bank, he found 17 pennies, 4 nickels, 5 dimes, and 2 quarters. What was the value of the coins?

Calculate values:

  • 17 pennies = \( 17 \times 1 = 17\cent \)
  • 4 nickels = \( 4 \times 5 = 20\cent \)
  • 5 dimes = \( 5 \times 10 = 50\cent \)
  • 2 quarters = \( 2 \times 25 = 50\cent \)

Total: \( 17 + 20 + 50 + 50 = 137\cent \)

Answer: 137 cents (or $1.37)

Problem 25
The answer to 52 divided by 9 is not a whole number. What whole number represents a reasonable estimate of the answer? Explain why you chose that number.

Multiples of 9 are: 9, 18, 27, 36, 45, 54.

52 is very close to 54. Since \( 54 \div 9 = 6 \), 6 is the best estimate.

Answer: 6 (Because 6 × 9 is 54, which is close to 52)

Problem 30
Show all the different ways these bills can be arranged in a row: One $1 bill, One $5 bill, One $10 bill.

This is a permutation of 3 items. There are 6 possible ways:

  • $1, $5, $10
  • $1, $10, $5
  • $5, $1, $10
  • $5, $10, $1
  • $10, $1, $5
  • $10, $5, $1
