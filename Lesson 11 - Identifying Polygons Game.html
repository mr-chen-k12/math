Lesson 11: What is a Polygon?

A fun introduction for 6th Graders!

The Two Simple Rules of Polygons

A polygon is a flat, 2D shape with straight sides that are all connected to make a closed path. That's it! To be a polygon, a shape must follow two simple rules:

  • Rule 1: It must have only straight sides. No curves allowed!
  • Rule 2: It must be a "closed" shape. No gaps or openings!

Examples of Polygons

Triangle

3 straight sides. Closed. It's a polygon!

Square

4 straight sides. Closed. It's a polygon!

Pentagon

5 straight sides. Closed. It's a polygon!

Hexagon

6 straight sides. Closed. It's a polygon!

Concave Polygon

Has a "dent" but still follows the rules! It's a polygon.

These are NOT Polygons

Curved Side

This shape has a curve, so it breaks Rule #1.

Open Shape

There is a gap! This breaks Rule #2 because it's not closed.

Crossing Sides

The sides cross over in the middle. Not a simple polygon!
