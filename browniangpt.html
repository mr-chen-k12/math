What should I notice?

Each particle moves unpredictably from moment to moment. But if you watch many particles, you can see a pattern: they tend to spread out over time.

\[ \langle r^2 \rangle \approx 4Dt \]

This says the average squared distance from the starting point grows with time. \(D\) is the diffusion coefficient.

1. The big idea

Brownian motion is the random movement of tiny particles suspended in a fluid. The particle looks like it is moving by itself, but it is really getting bumped again and again by smaller molecules.

2. Random does not mean useless

One particle is hard to predict. Many particles are easier to describe using averages. This is a powerful idea in science, data science, statistics, and AI.

\[ \Delta x \sim \text{random}, \quad \Delta y \sim \text{random} \]

3. Why it matters

Brownian motion helped scientists prove that atoms and molecules are real. It is also connected to diffusion, heat, probability, stock models, and random walks.
