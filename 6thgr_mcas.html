Greatest common factor

What is the greatest common factor of \(48\) and \(60\)?

Answer: C, \(12\).

Factors of \(48\) include \(1,2,3,4,6,8,12,16,24,48\). Factors of \(60\) include \(1,2,3,4,5,6,10,12,15,20,30,60\). The greatest factor they share is \(12\).

Powers of 3

Which expression has a value of \(243\)?

Answer: C, \(3^5\).

\(3^5=3\times3\times3\times3\times3=243\).

Ratios with marbles

A student has \(8\) marbles. \(3\) are green, and all the other marbles are yellow. Select the three true ratio statements.

Answers: B, D, and E.

There are \(3\) green marbles and \(8-3=5\) yellow marbles. Therefore, green to yellow is \(3:5\), yellow to all marbles is \(5:8\), and all marbles to green marbles is \(8:3\).

Point on a number line

Answer: B, \(\frac{7}{4}\).

The number line is divided into fourths. Point \(K\) is at \(1\frac{3}{4}\), which is \(\frac{7}{4}\).

Cupcake expressions

A bakery sells boxes of cupcakes. Each box costs \(\$5.00\). Answer all four parts.

A. What is the total cost of \(8\) boxes?

B. Write an expression for the total cost of \(c\) boxes.

C. A coupon gives \(\$2.50\) off one purchase. Write an expression for total cost of \(c\) boxes with coupon.

D. A teacher plans to spend \(\$47.50\) using the coupon. How many boxes can she purchase?

Answers:
A. \(8\times5=40\), so the cost is \(\$40\).
B. \(5c\).
C. \(5c-2.50\).
D. Solve \(5c-2.50=47.50\). Add \(2.50\): \(5c=50\). Divide by \(5\): \(c=10\). She can purchase 10 boxes.

Volume and mass of a prism

A rectangular prism block has dimensions \(4\) in. by \(12\) in. by \(2\) in. and mass \(4\) kg. A new shape has mass \(2\) kg. Which shape could not be the shape cut from the block?

Answer: D.

The original volume is \(4\times12\times2=96\) cubic inches. A mass of \(2\) kg is half of \(4\) kg, so the new shape should have half the volume: \(96\div2=48\) cubic inches. Choices A, B, and C each have volume \(48\). Choice D has volume \(2\times6\times1=12\) cubic inches, so it could not have mass \(2\) kg.

Statistical question

A teacher asks students four questions about a school play. Which question is a statistical question?

Answer: A.

A statistical question expects answers that vary. Different students may have different favorite actors. The other choices ask for one fixed fact about the play.

Word expression

Consider the expression \(15n-9\). Which word expression is equivalent?

Answer: B.

\(15n\) means the product of \(15\) and \(n\). Then subtracting \(9\) means “nine less than” that product.

Surface area of a triangular prism

A net of a triangular prism is shown. What is the total surface area?

Answer: C, \(408\) square centimeters.

The three rectangles have areas \(15\times10=150\), \(15\times8=120\), and \(15\times6=90\). Their total is \(360\). Each triangle has area \(\frac12\times8\times6=24\), so two triangles have area \(48\). Total surface area: \(360+48=408\) square centimeters.

Coordinate Plane Distance

Point \(Q\) is shown on the coordinate plane. Which ordered pair represents a point that is exactly 4 units from point \(Q\)?

Answer: C, \((2,7)\).

Point \(Q\) is at \((2,3)\). The point \((2,7)\) has the same \(x\)-coordinate, so it is directly above \(Q\). The distance is \(7-3=4\) units.

Percents, Fractions, and States

Aaron visited 30% of the 50 states in the United States. Answer all four parts.

A. What fraction of the states did Aaron visit?

B. How many states did Aaron visit?

C. After 5 more states, what percent will he have visited?

D. After Part C, how many additional states are needed to reach exactly 76%?

Answers: A. \(\frac{3}{10}\), B. 15 states, C. 40%, D. 18 states.

Since \(30\%=\frac{30}{100}=\frac{3}{10}\), Aaron visited \(0.30\times50=15\) states. After 5 more, he will have visited \(15+5=20\) states. Since \(20\div50=0.40\), that is 40%. To reach 76%, he needs \(0.76\times50=38\) states total. From 20 states, he needs \(38-20=18\) more states.

Dividing Fractions

Which expression is equivalent to \(\frac{3}{4}\div\frac{2}{3}\)?

Answer: C, \(1\frac{1}{8}\).

Dividing by a fraction means multiplying by its reciprocal: \(\frac{3}{4}\div\frac{2}{3}=\frac{3}{4}\times\frac{3}{2}=\frac{9}{8}=1\frac{1}{8}\).

Rectangle on a Coordinate Plane

Hakeem plotted points \(S\), \(T\), and \(U\). Point \(V\) will be plotted so rectangle \(STUV\) is formed. What is the length of side \(UV\)?

Length of \(UV\):

Answer: 4 units.

The points are \(S(-2,-6)\), \(T(-2,-2)\), and \(U(6,-2)\). To make rectangle \(STUV\), point \(V\) must be at \((6,-6)\). Side \(UV\) goes from \((6,-2)\) down to \((6,-6)\), so the length is \(|-2-(-6)|=4\) units.

Mean, Median, Mode, and Range

Jared recorded these points: 2, 3, 4, 5, 7, 7, 9, 9, 9, 10. Which statement is true?

Answer: B.

There are 10 numbers, so the median is the average of the 5th and 6th numbers: \((7+7)\div2=7\). The range is the greatest minus the least: \(10-2=8\). The mode is 9, and the mean is \(65\div10=6.5\).

17

Rectangular Prism Built from Cubes

The prism is built from cubes with edge length \(\frac{1}{3}\) inch. Select the two correct statements.

Answers: C and D.

The prism is 5 cubes long, 4 cubes wide, and 3 cubes high. Each cube edge is \(\frac{1}{3}\) inch. So the width is \(4\times\frac{1}{3}=\frac{4}{3}\) inches, and the height is \(3\times\frac{1}{3}=1\) inch. Each small cube has volume \(\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^3=\frac{1}{27}\), not \(\frac{1}{6}\). The whole prism volume is \(\frac{5}{3}\times\frac{4}{3}\times1=\frac{20}{9}\) cubic inches, not \(\frac{20}{3}\).

18

Statistical Questions

A student will ask a sample of classmates questions about a local movie theater. Which are statistical questions? Select the three statistical questions.

Answers: B, D, and E.

A statistical question expects answers that vary from person to person. Classmates could give different answers for how many action movies they watched, how many movies they saw at the theater, and what snack they bought. The theater address and the start time of one specific movie are fixed facts, so they are not statistical questions.
